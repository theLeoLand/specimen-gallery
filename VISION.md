# Vision

## What Specimen Gallery Is

Specimen Gallery is an open, community-built library of high-quality, transparent-background specimen images. It exists to provide illustrators, educators, researchers, and designers with clean visual references — properly licensed, easy to find, and ready to use. Think of it as a shared commons for specimen cutouts.

## What It Is Not

- **Not a taxonomy authority.** We use GBIF for name validation, but we don't define or adjudicate taxonomy.
- **Not a social network.** No profiles, followers, or feeds. Contribution is the interaction.
- **Not a field guide or species database.** We complement those resources; we don't replace them.
- **Not an observation platform.** We care about the image as a reusable asset, not when/where it was taken.

## Core Principles

- **Clarity first.** Visual quality and usability matter more than feature count. If something adds confusion, it doesn't ship.
- **Simplicity over complexity.** Every feature must earn its place. We'd rather do less, well.
- **Open by default.** The platform, the code, and the content are meant to be shared and reused.
- **Honest representation.** Uncertainty is visible. Unverified IDs are labeled. Community consensus is transparent.
- **Evolving, not rigid.** We grow from real use, not speculation. Decisions are reversible where possible.
- **Lightweight moderation.** Community contribution with just enough structure to maintain quality.

## Evolution

The platform may grow to include APIs, integrations with educational tools, or expanded metadata. We're open to that evolution — but we won't over-specify a roadmap or promise features prematurely. Any additions must preserve the core values: clarity, simplicity, and openness. If something doesn't fit, we won't force it.

## Contribution Mindset

If you're contributing code, keep these in mind:

- **Prefer small PRs.** Incremental improvements are easier to review and less risky.
- **Optimize for clarity.** Readable code, intuitive UX, correct behavior — in that order.
- **Avoid big redesigns without discussion.** Open an issue first for structural changes.
- **Keep it simple.** If a feature needs extensive explanation, it's probably too complex.
- **Performance matters.** Server-rendered means every request counts.
- **When in doubt, leave it out.** We can always add later; removing is harder.
